Description

From 2011 to 2021 I lived in the Middle East. While there I was able to enjoy some of the most incredible food. Spicy mandis, cheesy manakesh, simple shawarmas, and, best of all, bright and lemony lentil soup.

This soup reminds me so much of my time in the Middle East. It has the deep warming flavor of the lentil soups I enjoyed there, with a fresh pop of lemony flavor to brighten it up. It is a light soup that won't leave you with a heavy feeling stomach, which makes it a good soup to enjoy all year round. It pairs well with yummy garlic bread, or with some cheesy manakesh.

This soup is quick and easy, with few ingredients. This makes it a snap to throw together on those busy days, and makes it very wallet friendly. A serving of this is enough for lunch or light dinner, but you can also pair it with a salad and bread for a more well rounded meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 Tablespoon oil
  • 1 medium white onion, diced
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 5 cloves of garlic, peeled and diced
  • 6 cups chicken or vegetable stock (or use 3 bullion cubes in 6 cups water)
  • 1 1/2 cups red lentils
  • 1 small can of corn (about 1 cup)
  • 2 teaspoons cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • juice of half to whole lemon, depending on taste

Directions

  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat, then add onions and carrots and saute for about 5 minutes until onions are translucent
  2. Add garlic and saute 1 minute more, or until fragrent
  3. Add stock, lentils, corn, cumin and curry powder to the pot and bring to a simmer
  4. Simmer for 15 minutes,stirring occasionally, until lentils are tender
  5. Use an immersion blender and blend soup until smooth
  6. Add lemon juice and mix, and a pinch of sea salt and pepper to taste
